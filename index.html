ARCS Model and Its Application in E-learning

The ARCS Model, developed by John M. Keller, explains how learner motivation can be intentionally designed through Attention, Relevance, Confidence, and Satisfaction. This website demonstrates how the model can be applied in e-learning environments.

Figure: ARCS components function as an integrated motivational system.

Overview of the ARCS Model

Rather than assuming motivation occurs naturally, the ARCS Model provides a structured framework for designing instructional experiences that capture, sustain, and reinforce learner motivation—especially important in online learning contexts.

Figure: Full ARCS framework including major components and design considerations.

About the Author

John M. Keller is an educational psychologist known for his work on motivational design. His ARCS Model has been widely adopted in instructional design, corporate training, and e-learning due to its practical structure and strong theoretical foundation.
